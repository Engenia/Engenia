Both nozzles must touch the sprocket faces. If they don't, oil will only feed from one side - the path of least resistance. There must be back-pressure for oil to feed evenly on both faces. Heat each tube and bend it to fit.

Delivery Bracket Drawing (click to view)
This bracket allows for the full axle movement. As delivered, the adjustment bolts and nuts only allow a limited movement of about 20mm.

Delivery Bracket Installed
You'll notice I have trimmed the rear of the bracket to fit around the weld. I should have used just a little more clearance, as it doesn't quite sit flush.

The Tornado is now running a 15 tooth front & a 43 tooth rear sprocket. The initial Pro-Oiler table selected was #14, but this tended to deliver too much oil when setting #3 is selected. The leaner table #15 is currently being assessed.
Due to a lack of space, I discarded the Pro-Oiler junction box, and devised my own connection method. 12V was picked up from the Speed Sensor cable, and a separate ground was taken to the rear of the engine - the same point that was used for the second negative battery cable.
